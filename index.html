MICROSCOPE


A microscope is a laboratory instrument that can be used to examine cells and other tiny objects.
Through one lens in the microscope, an objectâ€™s picture is enlarged. This lens bends light in a way that causes it to look as though an object is larger than it actually is.

A microscope enlarges the vision of an object by magnifying an object twice with the objective and oculus lenses.

The magnifying power of a simple microscope is given by:

M = 1 + D/F

Where,
D = the least distance of distinct vision
F = focal length of the convex lens



  • Mount a slide containing the specimen on top of the stage.

  • The specimen is illuminated by light coming from below.

  • A close-positioned objective lens creates a "real image" of the specimen.

  • Somewhere between the two lenses is where this image is produced.

  • The oculus lens examines the actual image, which has already been magnified, and produces a "virtual image" of it that has been numerously magnified.

  • This image is constructed from a great distance, behind the actual objective.

  • The final image is always inverted.


  • Clean microscope after each use.

  • Use lens paper to polish dirty lenses.

  • Don't exert pressure using a cloth because the lenses are quite delicate.

  • Clean the eyepiece using an alcohol-based wipe after properly wiping out the stage (the platform that contains the slides).

  • Carefully handle glass slides. If a slide breaks, make sure the contents are disposed of correctly.

  • When not in use, turn off the light source for the microscope. This will improve lamp longevity and save energy.

  • Always use two hands to carry the microscope, one to support the base and the other to grasp the arm.

  • Place the objective lenses on the lowest setting, lower the nosepiece, and turn off the light source to properly store the microscope.

  • Add a dust jacket to the cover.
